    Reflection

    CSS can become confusing the more you add different rules and commands. It can mess up what you are trying to do properly. The amount of time it takes to fix it, if you are not too sure of yourself, is ridiculous. Know the syntax and what it does, that will make understanding and working with the cascades so much easier. Wireframe one was me just fleshing out what a web page looks like, for the title page I focused mainly on title, content boxes (for whatever it may be) and a bulletin board because I wanted to be lots of random advertising on my characters page. (like some webpages today, how would advertising have looked back then. 1887) With Wireframe two, as you can see, I decided that I did not need any unnecessary content blocks and should focus mainly on overall theme of site, including bulletin boards and images. I tried to match up with wireframe one as much as I could, but some things worked better and other things had not, it all depends on what works and what does not.
